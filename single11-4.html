From Offer to Closing: An Insider’s Guide to the Buying Process

November 4, 2016

In Buyers

The most common question that I hear once my buyer’s offer has been accepted is: “What happens next?” Here are your SparkNotes to keep you on track:

Home Inspection

A home inspection is like getting a physical checkup on your new home. As your Realtor®, I will schedule a home inspector to thoroughly inspect everything from the roof to the foundation. Within 24-48 hours you will get a detailed report of the inspector’s findings. From there, I will guide you through negotiating repairs with the seller.

Lender Documents

I will send your mortgage lender the contract so they can begin the mortgage process. Communicate with your lender and promptly provide any documentation in a timely manner. Keep in mind, if you stall or neglect to acknowledge documents, it can delay closing.

Home Owners Insurance

Your lender will require you to have a Home Owner’s Insurance Policy to protect your investment. I suggest shopping around and talking to at least three companies to compare coverage.

Turn on Utilities

You will want to get utilities turned on or transferred in your name prior to closing. I have a list of utility contact numbers here.

Final Walkthrough

Before closing, I will schedule a final walkthrough for us to verify all repairs have been completed and the home is move in ready.

As always, I will be here every step of the way to remind you of deadlines, follow up with the listing agent and answer your questions.
