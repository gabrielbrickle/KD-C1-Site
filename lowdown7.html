Lowcountry Lowdown:  May Real Estate News & Events

In Lowcountry Lowdown

Hooray for May! May is upon us and that brings flowers and Spoleto. Now approaching its 41st season, Spoleto Festival USA is internationally recognized as America’s premier performing arts festival. You won’t want to miss the 17-day world class entertainment beginning May 26.

The economy in Charleston is terrific and continues to drive the real estate market. Let’s just look at a couple of recent news items other than Boeing, Volvo and Silicon Harbor. NCGS, a company that conducts clinical trials, will build their corporate headquarters here, investing $10 million and adding 80 jobs. The Port had its busiest month ever in March-- up 13% from last year. Summerville- based Scout Boats plans to invest $11 million to expand its facilities and create 370 jobs. Elsewhere in SC, BMWis reporting the best March ever and that sales of the Spartanburg-produced X5 have jumped 20.7%.  The NCAA has announced that SC will host 7 championships between 2019 and 2022. Look at this economy thrive!

As for real estate, through the first quarter compared to the first quarter of 2016, sales were up 13.6%. Median sales price is $245k, up 4.5%.  It’s becoming very difficult to find a single-family home in Mt Pleasant or James Island for under $300k. Mt Pleasant median sales price is now over $200/sqft. James Island is around $185/sqft. West Ashley is over $150/sqft. The Summerville/Goose Creek areas are the least expensive, but are now over $100/sqft. New construction is rampant all over. They are building a new exit on I-26 to accommodate all the new growth. This past week we saw almost 100 condo/townhome sales (50-60 is more customary). It’s a great time for Charleston!
