Lowcountry Lowdown: Lowcountry Lowdown: February

February 6, 2017

In Charleston Lifestyle

February may be one of Charleston’s coldest months, but the real estate market is sizzling hot! January 2017 was the busiest January we have EVER had. People ask me all the time if they should wait until spring to put their house on the market or buy. In some parts of the nation I'd say, "yes, there are better seasons for buying or selling." Charleston, however, is a different animal. It's no surprise that demand is high and will remain so year-round. After all, we live in the Best City in the World!

Volvo is going to open its plant in the Goose Creek/Summerville area. You can still buy a home there for $100/sqft. Of course, Mt. Pleasant is the most desirable area, and around $200/sqft. New mayor, John Tecklenburg, seems intent on reviving West Ashley. You can buy there for under $150/sqft. Of course, new construction is everywhere these days, but Johns Island is now on everyone’s radar.

Fourth quarter median price trends from 2015 vs 2016:

  • Goose Creek/Summerville $195k to $211k (8% increase)
  • Mount Pleasant $390k to $438k (12% increase)
  • West Ashley $250k t0 $257k (3% increase)
