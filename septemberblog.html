Lowcountry Lowdown:  September Real Estate News & Events

In Lowcountry Lowdown

September is in full swing!

We can anticipation a beautiful fall here in the Holy City and September has a great list of events coming up, including Charleston Restaurant Week & Charleston Beer Week. We are keeping an eye on Hurricane Irma and everyone is preparing per the Hurricane Checklist- -  please let me know if there is anything I can do to help you and your family.

The total eclipse brought a lot more national attention to Charleston-- not that we really needed it. Being voted the No. 1 City in the U.S. so many times has taken care of that! Our real estate market has responded by building new homes all over the Tri-County area. New construction is certainly a favorite among buyers. Currently 1/3 of the homes under contract are new construction. In certain areas, the percentage is well over 50%. Year to date in 2017, we are up 6.8% in number of transactions over the same time period of 2016. It looks like we may sell 18k homes for the first time in our history. Median sales price is up a modest 4.9% to an all time high of $255k. Many people are thinking our market is overheated, but a 4.9% appreciation rate is just above the national average. A booming port city like Charleston could easily be appreciating faster than that. Credit has relaxed a bit, but is still very tight in general. 

The West Ashley area is now getting a Whole Foods! Our new mayor has made West Ashley’s revival a priority. A local group has purchased the Citadel Mall area and should be announcing some big changes there soon. Mt Pleasant continues to be the most popular place in all of Charleston. Goose Creek is really starting to see a boom with the coming of Volvo. As always, let me know if you or anyone you know needs a trustworthy and dependable realtor. I am here to help!
