Neighborhoods

Old Village

The Old Village is one of the areas most coveted neighborhoods. Tucked off Coleman Boulevard in the area known as “Old Mt Pleasant,” this location cannot be beat. You can feel the history pulsing through the community as live oaks pour over the streets dotted with updated homes, parks and churches. You will probably be greeted with a wave from someone driving by in a golf cart. Because The Old Village is not a private community or a gated subdivision, its amenities are open for public use and there is no HOA fee.

Popular spots include: Alhambra Hall where you’ll see dog lovers unite by day and a wedding event venue by night. Pitt Street Pharmacy is another villager stop and has been for 75 years. Here, patrons can pick up prescriptions, but mostly you’ll see children sitting on stools at the soda fountain for a malted milkshake or grilled cheese sandwich. Another OV favorite is Pitt Street Bridge, which was once the connector from Mt. Pleasant to Sullivan’s Island over Cove Inlet and now serves as a wonderful 3-mile (there and back) walking and running path.

Folly Beach

Where it is always island time! Nestled between the Folly River and the Atlantic Ocean, and just 15 miles from downtown Charleston, Folly Beach is a way of life that’s simply and distinctly, only Folly. Surf the waves, drink in the Morris Island Lighthouse, fish off the pier or explore the wildlife… it’s high tides and good vibes here.

The Inside Scoop: Skip siesta and say fiesta! Head over to Taco Boy for a refreshing and light “Original Frozen Driver,” a frozen screwdriver made with freshly squeezed OJ.

Sullivan's Island

Sullivan’s Island promises that time spent here is a time remembered. Whether you are soaking up Revolutionary War history at Fort Moultrie, soaking up the sunshine on the beach, or kiteboarding along the shore, there is something for everyone. Nestled at the mouth of the Charleston Harbor and neighborhood to Isle of Palms, the 3.3-mile stretch invites travelers from all over the country.

The Inside Scoop: Locals and history buffs alike know that Edgar Allan Poe was stationed at Fort Moultrie and his time on the island inspired “The Gold Bug.” Today stands the legendary Poe’s Tavern, a laid-back oceanfront restaurant serving delicious burgers. Grab a bite!
