Neighborhoods

Old Village

Old Village – the areas most coveted neighborhood. Tucked off Coleman Boulevard in the area known as “Old Mount Pleasant,” this location cannot be beat. You can feel the history pulsing through the community as live oaks pour over the streets dotted with updated homes, parks and churches. You will probably be greeted with a wave from someone driving by in a golf cart. Notably, Old Village is not a private community or a gated subdivision, its amenities are open for public use, and there is no HOA fee.

Get The Inside Scoop!

Old Village Inside Scoop:

Check out these popular neighborhood spots: Alhambra Hall a dog lovers’ oasis by day and an events venue by night, most popular for weddings. Pitt Street Pharmacy dating back over 75 years, villagers pick up prescriptions and children sit on stools at the soda fountain for a malted milkshake or grilled cheese sandwich. With gorgeous views of the marsh and picturesque sunsets, Pitt Street Bridge serves as a wonderful 3-mile (there and back) walking and running path that was once the connector from Mount Pleasant to Sullivan’s Island over Cove Inlet.

Folly Beach

Folly Beach –where it is always island time. Nestled between the Folly River and the Atlantic Ocean, and just 15 miles from downtown Charleston, Folly Beach is a way of life that’s simply and distinctly, only Folly. Surf the waves, drink in the Morris Island Lighthouse, fish off the pier, or explore the wildlife… it’s high tides and good vibes here.

Get The Inside Scoop!

Folly Beach Inside Scoop:

Skip siesta and say fiesta! Head over to Taco Boy for a refreshing and light “Original Frozen Driver,” a frozen screwdriver made with freshly squeezed OJ.

Sullivan's Island

Sullivan’s Island – treasured for its history and folklore. Whether you are soaking up Revolutionary War history at Fort Moultrie, basking in the sunshine on the beach, or kiteboarding along the shore, there is something for everyone. Sitting at the mouth of the Charleston Harbor, a little less than 10 miles east of downtown, and neighbors to Isle of Palms, the 3.3-mile stretch invites travelers from all over the country.

Get The Inside Scoop!

Sullivan's Island Inside Scoop:

Edgar Allan Poe was stationed at Fort Moultrie and his time on the island inspired, “The Gold Bug.” Today stands the legendary Poe’s Tavern, a laid-back oceanfront restaurant serving delicious burgers.
