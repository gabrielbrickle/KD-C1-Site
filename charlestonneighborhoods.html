Neighborhoods

Old Village

Old Village can easily be described as Mount Pleasant’s most coveted neighborhood. Minutes away from Sullivan’s Island and a bike ride to Shem Creek, this location cannot be beat. Updated homes, parks and churches sprinkle the neighborhood, as live oaks pour over the streets. Don’t be surprised if you’re greeted with a wave from someone driving by in a golf cart. The charm of the neighborhood is contagious. Conveniently, the amenities in Old Village are open for public use and there are no HOA fees.

Get The Inside Scoop!

Old Village Inside Scoop:

Check out these popular neighborhood spots: Alhambra Hall is a dog lovers’ oasis by day and an events venue by night. Pitt Street Pharmacy is where villagers pick up prescriptions and children eat ice cream at the soda fountain. Pitt Street Bridge is a wonderful 3-mile roundtrip walking and running path that once connected Mount Pleasant to Sullivan’s Island over Cove Inlet.

Folly Beach

Folly is a way of life that’s simply and distinctly, only Folly. This funky, laid-back, come-as-you-are kind of beach is packed with things to do. Surf the waves, drink in the Morris Island Lighthouse, fish off the pier, or explore the sea-wildlife. Downtown Folly features an eclectic array of locally owned shops, restaurants and lots of live music. You’ll relish in the legendary sunset views. It’s high tides and good vibes here.

Get The Inside Scoop!

Folly Beach Inside Scoop:

Skip siesta and say fiesta! Head over to Taco Boy for a refreshing and light “Original Frozen Driver,” a frozen screwdriver made with freshly squeezed OJ.

×

Sullivan's Island

Whether you are soaking up Revolutionary War history at Fort Moultrie, basking in the sunshine on the beach, or kiteboarding along the shore, there is something for everyone on Sullivan’s Island. Neighbors to Isle of Palms, the 3.3-mile beach is perfectly lined with historic homes, many that have been renovated over the years, but some that have remained treasured beach homes through generations of Charlestonians.

Get The Inside Scoop!

Sullivan's Island Inside Scoop:

Edgar Allan Poe was stationed at Fort Moultrie and his time on the island inspired, “The Gold Bug.” Today stands the legendary Poe’s Tavern, a laid-back oceanfront restaurant serving delicious burgers.

×

Mt. Pleasant

Just across the harbor and over the Ravenel Bridge, you’ll find the sought-after town of Mount Pleasant. Acclaimed for its quality of life, historic neighborhoods and award-winning schools, Mount Pleasant is admired for it’s convenience to everything. Paddle board on Shem Creek, marvel at the shrimp boats that line the banks or indulge in fresh seafood. Mount Pleasant offers golfing, parks and marinas, bicycle paths and spectacular waterfront views. You’ll see tourists and locals alike walking the Ravenel Bridge, and even snapping pictures of the best views of the city. Mount Pleasant is where recreation meets lifestyle.

Get The Inside Scoop!

Mt.Pleasant Inside Scoop:

Sign up for the Cooper River Bridge Run, the third largest 10k in the United States. It’s not just a race, it’s an experience.

×

Daniel Island

Daniel Island is a master-planned community surrounded by rivers, creeks and beautiful marsh views. With more than 25 miles of trails, nationally ranked golf courses and hundreds of acres of parks, it’s a destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Hop on a bike to enjoy the many shops and restaurants that line Daniel Island Drive and watch out, you may spot an alligator or two sunbathing by your neighborhood pond!

Get The Inside Scoop!

Daniel Island Inside Scoop:

Check out the award-winning Volvo Car Stadium, which hosts a variety of events throughout the year - everything from concerts to the Volvo Car Open, the largest WTA tournament in North America.

×

West Ashley

West Ashley, formerly known as “West of the Ashley,” is one of Charleston’s oldest suburbs bordered by the Ashley River and intracoastal waterway. Rich in history, this area dates back to 1670, where English colonists established the first permanent European settlement in the Carolinas. Now Charles Towne Landing is a state historic site that visitors can tour and enjoy daily. Residents love West Ashley for the affordability, proximity to downtown and marsh and waterfront properties.

Get The Inside Scoop!

West Ashley Inside Scoop:

Exercise on the West Ashley Greenway, which takes you on a 10.5-mile (one way) ride from suburban Charleston west to the scenic Lowcountry wetlands that surround the city.
